Fatty Liver Disease 🍂

Characterised by excessive triglyceride accumulation within hepatocytes. Associated with alcohol intake (AFLD) or metabolic risk factors (NAFLD). May progress to NASH, fibrosis, and cirrhosis.

Hepatitis 🦠

Inflammation of liver parenchyma from viral infection (Hepatitis A–E), autoimmune processes, or toxic exposures. Chronic Hepatitis B and C are leading causes of cirrhosis and hepatocellular carcinoma.

Cirrhosis & HCC 🔬

Advanced irreversible fibrosis with disrupted hepatic architecture. Complications include portal hypertension, variceal bleeding, and hepatocellular carcinoma (HCC). Surveillance every 6 months recommended.
